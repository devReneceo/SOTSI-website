The site is attracting more people than ever — sessions are up 55% year over year — but it is holding far fewer of them. Engagement has fallen from 52% to 32%, bounce has climbed to 68%, and average time on site has nearly halved (219s → 122s). The data points to one conclusion: the platform can no longer keep up with the audience the brand is earning.

How the current site is costing you

Engagement collapsed while traffic grew. Sessions rose +55% YoY, yet engaged sessions actually fell (38,623 → 36,613). More visitors, fewer staying.
Your largest audience is mobile — 56% of sessions — and that's exactly where the site is slowest (18.5s to show the main content) and the menu is hidden.
The home page is the #1 entry point (47,975 sessions, 41% of all) and ~70% bounce — the single highest-leverage page, and the weakest.
Your best-quality channel — organic search (56% engagement) — is only 23% of traffic, capped by the site's SEO gaps.
AI search is essentially zero: 9 sessions from AI assistants in a year — the fastest-growing way people discover brands, and the site isn't built to be cited.
Only 2.8% of sessions complete a key action — the structure isn't guiding visitors toward what matters.

What we propose to improve

A fast site for the mobile majority. Rebuild targets LCP <2.5s (from 18.5s) and <3MB pages — serving the 56% of visits on phones first.
A home that holds visitors. The redesign rebuilds the exact page 47,975 people land on first — clearer message, guided flow — to cut the 70% bounce.
Grow your best channel. Built-in SEO fixes target organic search — your highest-engaging traffic (56%) — to lift it past today's 23% share.
Open the AI-search channel. FAQ schema + entity data for Gary Zukav make the site citable by ChatGPT, Perplexity & Google AI — a channel sending just 9 sessions today.
Guide visitors to act. Clear, single CTAs per section target the 2.8% key-action rate — turning attention into enrollments, sign-ups, and donations.
A secure, low-maintenance platform. Webflow removes the plugin attack surface (the current site already shows an unauthorized injection) and the hosting / backup burden.
The one-line case: the brand is earning more traffic than ever, but a slow, hard-to-read, search-invisible platform is letting it slip away — most of it on mobile, most of it on the home page. The new site is engineered to keep the audience you already have, and to be found by the audience you're missing.
