The home page ships 22.7 MB across 146 requests, and takes 18.5 seconds to show its main content on a phone. On desktop it scores an acceptable 89, but because 56% of visitors are on mobile (see the Analytics brief), the slow mobile experience is what most people actually get — and the Core Web Vitals Google uses for ranking fail there.

Why the page is this heavy

Video dominates the page. 14.2 MB of video (8 files) auto-load — nearly two-thirds of the entire page weight.
Images aren't optimized. 5.8 MB of images; modern formats (WebP/AVIF) and right-sizing would save ~3.9 MB.
Too many files. 56 stylesheets and 46 scripts load for one page — the hallmark of a plugin-heavy WordPress build.
Render-blocking resources. CSS/JS in the way of first paint cost an estimated 2.3s before anything useful appears.
Legacy code & weak caching. ~76 KB of legacy JavaScript is shipped, and better caching could save another ~167 KB.
Third-party load. 1.6 MB across 27 third-party requests — each one outside your control and a point of failure.

Four of five Core Web Vitals fail on mobile — including LCP, the metric Google weights most heavily. Only layout stability (CLS) is in range: the page is visually steady, but agonizingly slow to appear.

What the rebuild targets

Right-sized, lazy media. Replace 14.2MB of auto-loading video with a poster + click-to-play, compressed stream — the single biggest win.
Modern images. WebP/AVIF + correct sizing recovers ~5.2MB and sharpens quality on every device.
One platform, not 100 files. Webflow ships lean CSS/JS — no plugin pile-up of 56 stylesheets and 46 scripts.
Targets that pass. Engineering goals: LCP < 2.5s, page < 3MB, validated on the live build before go-live.
Managed CDN & caching. Global delivery and long-cache assets included — no hosting tuning to maintain.
Faster pages rank higher. Passing Core Web Vitals lifts the same SEO the Analytics brief shows is your best-engaging channel.
